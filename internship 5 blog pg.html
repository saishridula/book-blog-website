BEST FALL BOOKS - MY FAV AUTUMN READS

By Sai shridula

It is not easy to narrow down a list of the best fall books. The chilly, colorful world outside and the cozy indoors are so irresistibly luring, inviting us to make some tea, grab a snuggly

oo

To Kill a Mockingbird by Harper Lee

Set in the racially tense American South, this novel follows young Scout Finch as her father, Atticus, defends a Black man wrongly accused of assault. It explores deep themes of justice, morality, and childhood innocence

Pride and Prejudice by Jane Austen

Elizabeth Bennet navigates love, class, and society in Georgian England. Her witty exchanges with the aloof Mr. Darcy are iconic. The novel balances romance with sharp social commentary. It explores the limitations placed on women during the time. Austen’s wit and charm make it endlessly enjoyable.

1984 by George Orwell

A dystopian tale set in a totalitarian regime where the government watches every move. Winston Smith begins to question the reality fed to him by “Big Brother.” Themes of surveillance, freedom, and truth are powerfully explored. It’s a chilling warning against unchecked political power. Still eerily relevant today.
